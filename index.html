What is s/f/wønderland?

s/f/wønderland is a web-based painting tool that explores fandom cultures as a transgressive and creative space for queer liberation, particularly in the context of state-enforced media censorship in Malaysia and Singapore.

How it works

  1. Select a base template.
  2. Play with brushes, stamps, backgrounds, filters and text - let your imagination run wild!
  3. Save your masterpiece - or share it with the world!

Background

given the gap in literature and documentation regarding queer communities* in places i grew up (Malaysia and Singapore), i wanted this project to explore queer existence from the perspective of social media and digital community creation. in particular, i was interested in how queer people learned about and later navigate queerness in a local media landscape + social infrastructure that denies lgbtq+ people of any positive representation nor legal protections**.

during the spring break, i spoke with 6 friends who: (1) identified somewhere under the queer umbrella, AND (2) had experiences growing up in Malaysia and/or Singapore.

all of us happened to be born in the 90s or early 2000s, and began using the internet in our late-primary ~ early-secondary school days.

through our conversations, we learned that most of us first came into contact with the notion of queerness through fan fiction and queer fandom pairings on the internet - many shared experiences reading slash on fanfic.net, in particular - which provided an alternative source of knowledge and guidance beyond parental figures and local media + educational institutions. as such, for my capstone project, i wanted to explore the (re)imagination of queerness* through media appropriations of censored content as a form of creative resistance against oppressive state-enforced infrastructures. while every participant has their own understanding of the role queer fandoms play in the broader political environment**, most believe that the transgressive nature of queer fan-made narratives and worlds are not merely avenues for escapism, but are also capable of shifting attitudes and perceptions concerning queerness on a broader societal level.

other research references / interests included Chaoyang Trap’s article on Chinese fanfic writers navigating post-AO3 ban era and cartographic charts marking queer presence + history in Queering The Map and Pink Dot SG’s “Love Lives Here.”

while this project is primarily made with my participating friends in mind, it is also available for other people who are familiar with / grew up with / dare i say, thrived through queer fandoms, particularly when they are unable to enjoy such queer freedoms openly in their own environment due to fear of prosecution, social stigma etc.
